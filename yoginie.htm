PROBLEM STATEMENT

How might we help people get access to professional yoga class that also fits their busy schedule?

I had the idea since 2015, when I solo traveled widely in Europe. Travelers would not want to miss any sightseeing spot, so did I. I tried to keep daily schedule as tight as possible but still wanted to squeeze a quick but nice Yoga practice in the morning right before hectic adventures.Sadly, I could not find any mobile app that provide proper yoga guidance which can be customized into different duration and styles to suit my schedule.

SOLUTION

A customizable yoga app that can provide tailored duration and types of yoga

Few years later, I not only completed my 200 hours Yoga Teacher Training but also have the idea to make an app to help more busy on-the-road people to involve more Yoga practice.

2019 Redesign

Brainstorming

I used mind map as a brainstorming tool. Yoginie contains the concepts of minimalism, customization, yoga techniques and supportive community. With the brainstorming, I concluded that the targeted users should be as following: traveler and busy people (including those who cannot go to the gym because they are always on the road), beginners to intermediate yogis (those who are experienced in Yoga probably do not need the guidance), people who love minimalist style, and basically everyone who needs yoga guidance.

User Research

To empathise more with the users, I started the user research with user feedback interview and my understanding of the culture yogis.

In the User Feedback Interview, I divided the interview into three parts. First, I built the imaginary use case for them and asked them about their past travelling experiences and workout pattern. Second, I showed them the app “Yoga Studio” and observed their behaviour. Last, I showed them my paper prototype, observed how they interacted with my paper prototype, and asked their expectation.

Visual Design

Calm + True Self

I chose blue, grey and beige as primary colours because blue brings calmness and beige is a neutral and naked colour, which is the way I believe how yoga transforms people—leading people to the purest self.

 

Flower of Life

The inspiration of the logo is “Flower of Life” (the left one). Flower of Life is a token composing multiple evenly-shaped and overlapping circles, which are arranged in a flower-like pattern in a hexagon. The logo has been regarded as a sacred geometry for centuries. With its harmoniously shaped circles, “Flower of Life” delivers messages of the endless life cycle and the harmonious energy pattern on the Earth.

Inspired by Flower of Life, I use four octagons to shape the logo, and three lines form the letter "Y", which means "Yoginie". I choose geometrical shape as the base of the logo to deliver the idea of natural harmony. I wish every user would receive the sense of harmonious beauty upon entering the app. Besides, I chose to integrate the blue and beige in the logo to create a message of calm and peace.

User Interface

I designed the User Interface of the app, and the main colour is the blue with HEX #B6DCE3. I want to convey a concept of harmony and elegance. Harmony and elegance are also the two emotions I wish all of the users could feel the same way about themselves when or after using Yoginie.

 

What I learned

This is my very first UX project after I have made up my mind to switch my career. Thus, it is not a perfect project but it recorded the process of how I self-learned UX design by practical experiences. There are a few things I realized through my very first UX project.

  1. 🗣Just talk to the users

    I didn’t know how to start it and thus grabbed a chance to ask someone I met who graduated from University of Washington with a degree in HCI. She told me just to go talk to the users, ask as many people as I could to gather user insight.

  2. 📊The importance of visualising information

    As a designer, to reach the balance of visualization idea and jotting down thinking process is important. However, in the beginning, I recorded this project with mostly texts, which didn’t reflect my knowledge about appropriately utilizing graphs and visualization tools to reach the logic and aesthetic thinking process.

  3. 💭The WHY in the product

    According to the bestselling author Simon Sinek of Start With Why, to establish a strong solid WHY for your business is the most crucial thing to do because it is going to drive you to WHAT you product in the end. I was asked a lot for why I chose this topic as my very first side project, and I didn’t know the WHY is a crucial essence in a product before.

Looking back, It’s my very first project, so many things in the project are not mature enough. However, since yoga is my passion, I would like to list what I will do to improve this project.

  1. 👥Interview more people

    Looking back, I feel like the interview I did back then was not enough in terms of interview numbers. I followed the N/N Group’s suggestion on testing with 5 people, but the piece of suggestion was for testing, which I didn’t realize back then.

  2. 📱Improve the prototyping

    My skills in handling the prototyping software Principle was not polished at all. However, I managed to make it to get a sense of the product interaction. I definitely would want to redo the prototyping and make the interaction smoother.

  3. 🧪Do more testing

    If I had more chances and time, I want to then test the app with the prototype I made to test the functionality and interaction with more users to collect feedback. Feedback is important for improvement for the product and myself, so I definitely want to get more feedback.

What will I improve?
