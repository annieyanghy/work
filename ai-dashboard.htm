My mission

As the UX designer at MoBagel Inc., my major workload is to redesign the user interface for Decanter AI, the main product that empowers data analysts with rapidly built Auto Machine Learning (AutoML) models.

AutoML, AI and Data Science are hard-core subjects, and my duty is to transform the technology into user-friendly product that can be easily used. It took me lots of efforts the beginning to first roughly understand what AutoML is. The specialty of designing an enterprise-faced product is that it focuses so much on the logic and the technology itself, instead of fancy and market-driven visual imagery.

Design process

I joined the product development process right before the phase of HI-fi UI and continued through the process to the alpha release. Before I joined, the Product Managers had had a blueprint for the totally renovating product release and I was referred to the company to help realize the UX renovation of the new release.

What had been done before I joined:

  1. User feedback interview: the product team had done interviews with some of our clients (real users) before I joined the company

  2. Brainstorming: based on the interviews with clients, the brainstorming sessions and ideas had been created before I joined

  3. Wireframes: wireframes for the product had been roughly sketched out

What was my mission:

  • To perfect the user flow

  • To flesh out the wireframe into hi-fi mockup

  • To perfect the documentation of the design handoff

  • To do testing on the mockup and collected user feedback by interviews

User flow chart

During this phase, I drafted up the user flow of the 7 main sections: Login Page. Model Builder, Experiments, Data Manager, Admin Setting, Projecting Setting, Nav Bar. This part of work is mainly for making sure there are no UI pages missing.

What I learned

Since this is the first time as a UX Designer, I learned tons in this project. Here I listed 3 main things I improved, which are mostly about the mindset.

  1. Confidence

    • In the beginning of the career transition, I was not confident in every decision I made and may sometimes doubt my own ability on UX design — Yes, the Imposter Syndrome (take a looks at how Times Magazine talked about it.)

    • How I gained confidence? Acknowledge my inadequacy and realize that everyone is learning.

  2. Never hesitate to ask WHY

    • In the beginning as I joined this AI startup, I was so intimidated by all of my colleagues and felt out of place because I seem to be the only one with a background in humanity and arts and no nothing about machine learning, data processing, and AI. So, I was very hesitant to ask Product Manager the reason of features he planed in the product.

    • How I started to ask why, proactively? As I gained more confidence in my work, I started to realize: asking the why is my job because I want to find out what users want!

  3. Don’t jump into the project TOO quickly

    • As I started to work as a UX Designer, I was very excited and didn’t think much about questioning the product rationale when I was given the wireframe of the product, because I naively assumed that my colleagues must know more about me in terms of machine learning. However, I was wrong! My colleagues did know more about me on regarding AI and machine learning, but I know human behavior more than they did and I had the precious angle as a beginner.
