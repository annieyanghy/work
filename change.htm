Project Status

Submitting to CHI 2020 Student Game Competition

 
 

Problem

People face difficulties to do more sustainable actions in daily life despite being aware of the importance of sustainability to various degrees.

Solution

An app that gamify habit forming process to motivate users take on more sustainable actions through the power of community and personal reflection.

The origin of the project came from my passion for sustainability. I have done other projects regarding sustainability and feels like there are more needs to be done, and one of which is the daily sustainable behaviour. I have observed a serious problem happening daily — the disposable eating utensils, which includes containers and cutlery. People eat out and use these disposable eating containers and other utensils automatically, without pausing and realizing that they can actually make a lot of change if they just bring a container to reuse. As a result, one of the goal of my master’s studies at the University of Michigan is to design a sustainable app that can help people develop sustainable habit, and I am also aiming to submit this project to CHI 2020 Student Game Competition.

Design Solution

 

1.Group Sustainable Challenge

Purpose: increase motivation by the sense of collaboration and competition

  • To increase the sense of community, we want to elevate the process of forming sustainable habits to a crowdsourcing game to not only motivate users to contribute to sustainable actions but also make them feel a sense of honor.

 
 
 
 

2. Community Map

Purpose: to connect local users to form a community

  • With CHANGE, we build a local map to help users to connect to other users, so as to create a sense of belonging. Also, from our user research, we know that users are greatly influenced by the environment and the community, so we try to build this app with a map that can help users to find nearby like-minded people who are also interested in cultivating sustainable habits.

 
 
 
 

3.Personal Challenge

Purpose: rewards and reflection to internalize sustainable habits

  • Apart from group challenges, we also create the section for solo-challenge-takers. In personal challenges, we focus on reflection through self-rating and assessment. Also, to incentivize users, we provide small real-ward rewards to motivate them so that they know their reflection with CHANGE is greatly connected to their daily life.

 

App Features

In the app, we designed two main features to help users to develop sustainable habits, one is Map and the other is Journey.

Design Process

Research

User Interview

To have a deeper understanding of people’s thoughts on sustainability and their current lifestyles, we interviewed 10 people that represented different stages of life, academic focuses, and cultural backgrounds. The interviewees include 1 Korean, 1 Chinese, 3 Taiwanese, and 5 Americans. All interviewees are between 20-50 years old and are currently living in the USA. However, most of them are young professionals or graduate students, who are a group of people we tried to target because they may still undergo a series of life changes, and might be more willing to develop new habits.

Before conducting the interviews, we developed interview protocols that focused on interviewees’ lunch journeys with three different overarching questions:

  • What is preventing people from developing sustainable habits? (Group A)

  • How do people currently practice sustainable habits? (Group B)

  • What sustainable habits do you find easy and/or challenging to implement? (Group C)

We started each interview with a set of general questions inquiring the interviewee’s experience in practising sustainable habits. According to the response, we made a quick decision to place our interviewee into one of the following groups: people who are unfamiliar with the concept of sustainability and rarely practice sustainable habits (Group A), people who are fully aware and are actively practising sustainable habits (Group B), and those who are somewhere in-between (GroupC). The result led each interviewee to follow-up questions designed for specific groups.

 

Affinity Wall

🛠 Sustainable Tools: Miro

After the user interviews, we collected insights from the notes together and analyzed the qualitative data with affinity wall. During the analysis process, we clustered the raw data with themes, which helped us to analyze the qualitative data more efficiently. One thing worth mentioning is that to make the project process sustainable too, we adopted a digital tool in the affinity wall stage to save the waste of physical post-it notes. We first took notes on the yellow digital notes, found common patterns among different notes, and grouped notes with the same patterns. We repeated the action several times until we reached critical findings.

 

Findings

The affinity wall unfolds the following three deciding factors that have a significant influence on people’s behaviors when they are trying to pursue a more sustainable lifestyle.

Convenience

People enjoy the convenience of buying takeout food and using disposable tableware, especially when they have busy schedules. According to our interviewees, it is way too convenient to use paper plates, and this is causing environmental problems since the campus produces a significant amount of disposable wastes every day. 

Desire to reduce living costs and a sense of guilt

We found that some people we talked to are consciously building sustainable habits such as bringing stainless steel straws to office, using energy-efficient lightbulbs, and collecting plastic containers for future use. Among them, some are driven by the desire to reduce living costs. Others, however, are driven by a sense of guilt after realizing that they have been throwing away one-time used containers and sandwich bags more frequently than they had imagined.

Support from the environment

Whether the environment provides support plays a crucial role. People care about how others judge them when they are trying to live a more sustainable life and feel more empowered when others around them are also striving to achieve the same goal. Oftentimes, they feel frustrated because they do not believe that individuals have the power to change the system or influence others. Among them, some feel helpless because of the overwhelming but unclear information on the Internet, and some blame themselves for being forgetful. Having little support from the environment, people give up before sustainable habits could be successfully developed.

We split the wireframes because we think it can best help us to focus on the logic and details of each smaller part. Design

From the following phase, I am in charge of all of the design as we are distributed with different tasks in order to submit to CHI 2020.
My task is user interface design and merge the design.

💪🏼My solo task: Merge Design

🛠 Tool used: Figma

Then, after the wireframes are reviewed, I was responsible for merging the wireframe and put them into high-fidelity design. Merging design is a considerable work, considering that everyone makes the design layout different and may use different paddings. As a result, to make the merging process efficient, I started by observing common components to create design components to be used in the whole app. After the design components were created, I could then used them in different app sections and easily edit them if I need a change. By starting off with design components, I can standardize the app design easily, mainly I need to merge design ideas from different people. At this stage, Figma helped greatly because I could take teammates’ work easily — through a shareable link! Also, I intensively used the newest featured released by Figma — Auto Layout, when I was making components, which helped me to quickly take care of paddings and any change in design.

 

💪🏼My solo task: Design System

I used Atomic Design approach when merging the different wireframes. I started by making the smallest components, like texts or icons, and then combine them into larger components such as modals or cards.

I used this approach to better systems the whole design styles, which also aids me to update multiple screens efficiently.

 

💪🏼My solo task: Interaction Design

🛠 Tool used: Figma

After the user interface is finished, I then started to design the interaction among screens. I still used Figma to prototype, and Smart Animate is the go-to option in the prototyping setting for me.
