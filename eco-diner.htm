Problem

If you are gluten-intolerant, it is very hard to find a friendly restaurant in Taipei because this knowledge— “gluten-free”— is still not that popular in Taiwan. Besides, eating plant-based healthy meal was still heavily limited to religious sakes in Taiwan, let alone eating in a sustainable and conscious way style.

Our approach

We are determined to change the dining landscape in Taipei by educating the customers with a whole new concept of eating and lifestyle. There were four of us in the beginning to prepare and organize the founding of the restaurant, which is a first organic, plant-based and gluten-free diner offering western holistic nutritious meals in Taipei.

Contribution to Sustainability

We try very hard to add ideas of sustainability into the dining experiences! It’s an add-on aside from the food customers pay for— it’s an idea that cab be embedded into the mind!

What I learned

From the 2-year adventure, I was really inspired to do more to make the world better. And I also gathered precious experiences and met wonderful people in the journey. 

The first lesson I learned is, empathy is crucial in a great design. For everything we succeeded or didn’t do that well all lies in sufficient empathy with users or lack of understanding. We were praised for creating a perfectly worry-free dining experience but also received negative feedback when we were making a radical movement on food waste. 

The second is, societal change takes time and patience. In the journey, frustration and disappointment are more than just familiar. The long lasting mentality of “bargain” products blind people from seeing the truth and appreciating the value. That’s why we try to challenge the society.  

In conclusion, there are numerous precious lessons I learned from the enlightening job experience, but I just named a few. It’s also the experiences that makes me want to further gain more knowledge and talents to make the world better in a more powerful way.
